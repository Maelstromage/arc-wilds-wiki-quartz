# Location: Bone Speaker Village

## Rumors
- A tribal settlement sits within Bone Speaker Jungle, anchored near the Lake of Bones.
- Outsiders say the villagers speak in a strange but highly structured tongue.
- Some say the village keeps sacred rites tied to spirits, water, and old powers in the jungle.
- A sacred cave near the village is said to bless worthy weapons.

## What is Known
- Bone Speaker Village is a tribal settlement within [[D&D/Campaigns/00 West Marches/World/Locations/Bone Speaker Jungle]].
- The people there speak Toki Pona.
- The village includes a large communal hut known as Tomo Suli where guests may sleep on woven grass beds.
- Tomo Suli serves as a shared lodging and gathering place for guests and villagers alike.
- The village also keeps a smaller water-house called Tomo Tello used for washing and bodily needs.
- The locals remove their footwear before entering the communal hut.
- The tribe can be warm, teasing, and highly social with outsiders once trust is established.
- The village keeps dogs, cooks shared meals, and treats T-Rex teeth as meaningful objects of value.
- Fenrir the cat is known in the village and is not especially patient with handling.
- Near the village is a sacred cave containing a circular pool fed by a carved stone face in the wall.
- Tiny drifting lights move through the cave like fireflies.
- The village is tied to a sacred cave and ritual pool where a spirit may test and bless those who come in respect.
- The tribe used this sacred pool to bless a spear made from a T-Rex tooth after the party proved themselves.
- Shae, one of the tribal leaders, served as a guide for the party toward the mountains.

## History
- The village served as a place of rest and translation for the party after they recovered Milo and made contact with the local people.
- During the party's stay, they slept in Tomo Suli, learned more Toki Pona, shared food with the villagers, and were brought into a sacred cave ritual.
- In that ritual, the tribe formed around the sacred pool, a spirit rose from the water, and the party was tested before the T-Rex tooth spear was blessed.
- The tribe later gifted the party the blessed spear and sent Shae with them as a guide.
